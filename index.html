About

I'm a soil scientist specializing in soil microbiology and environmental systems, with formal training in soil mapping, land evaluation, soil fertility assessment, and organic matter management. My work sits at the intersection of microbial ecology, soil health, and soil–plant–microbe interactions.

Day to day, that means a mix of laboratory and field-based work: microbial enumeration, enzyme activity assays, PCR and DNA extraction, physicochemical characterization, and organic waste stabilization. My research background spans soil quality assessment, bioremediation, and soil amendment evaluation.

Outside the lab, I'm teaching myself to bring computation into this work: Python and bioinformatics pipelines for diversity analysis, machine learning, and reproducible microbiome workflows, because a lot of what soil microbes are doing is easier to see once you can model it.

Bioinformatics · AMR Surveillance

AMR Resistome Pilot

AMR gene detection from real manure metagenomic sequencing data. Click for the summary.

A raw-reads-to-results pipeline detecting antimicrobial resistance (AMR) genes in real shotgun metagenomic sequencing data from livestock manure, using the CARD database and RGI (bwt mode). Compares resistomes between two real manure metagenome samples at both gene and drug-class level: quality control, alignment, and honest reporting of a low mapping rate, with no overstating what a small subsampled dataset can support. Later extended into a 12-sample statistically tested comparison (see next project).

Bioinformatics · AMR Surveillance

Manure Resistome Pipeline

12-sample manure vs. soil AMR comparison with Mann-Whitney U + FDR testing. Click for the summary.

Extends the pilot into a properly powered comparison: 12 shotgun metagenomic samples (6 manure, 6 soil) run through RGI/CARD alignment, merged into a gene × sample abundance matrix, then tested gene-by-gene with Mann-Whitney U and Benjamini-Hochberg FDR correction across all genes, so only statistically defensible differences are reported, not noise from testing hundreds of genes at once. PCA and a heatmap visualize which genes drive the separation between sources.

Bioinformatics · Comparative Genomics

PGPB Marker Gene Screening

BLASTp screening of 24 bacterial genomes for PGPB marker genes. Click for the summary.

A BLASTp-based pipeline screening 24 bacterial genomes (Bacillus, Pseudomonas, and Azospirillum species, plus outgroups) for three PGPB-associated marker genes: nifH (nitrogen fixation), pqqC (phosphate solubilization), and acdS (ACC deaminase/stress-ethylene modulation), against curated Swiss-Prot/NCBI reference sequences. Reciprocal BLAST caught a systematic false positive in Pseudomonas acdS hits, a cross-reaction within the broad PF00291 PLP-dependent enzyme family, filtered out before reporting genuine presence/absence calls.

Bioinformatics · 16S Amplicon Metagenomics

Compost Bucket Microbiome Comparison

Comparing microbial communities across two compost bucket designs — in development. Click for the summary.

Third in the pipeline series: real published 16S rRNA amplicon sequencing data comparing microbial communities across two compost bucket designs, conventional vs. a redesigned pyramid shape. Raw reads from 12 samples run through QIIME 2/DADA2 for per-sample error correction, paired-read merging, and chimera removal, producing a clean ASV table of exact sequence variants per sample — with six replicates per design, enough for genuine group-level hypothesis testing (PERMANOVA) on whether bucket geometry actually shifts community structure.
